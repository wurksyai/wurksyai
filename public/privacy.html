Privacy notice

Last updated: 27 November 2025

This privacy notice explains how Wurksy AI collects, uses and protects personal data when you use the Wurksy AI web application and related services (the “Service”).

When Wurksy AI is provided to you through a university or college, that institution will usually be the data controller. Wurksy AI then acts as a data processor on their behalf. For those deployments, you should also read your institution’s own privacy notice.

Who we are and how to contact us

Wurksy AI is currently developed and operated in the United Kingdom by its creator (the “Operator”). For institutional deployments, the university or college that licenses Wurksy AI will normally be the organisation responsible for your data.

If you have any questions about this notice or how your data is used, you can contact:

  • Email: info@wurksyai.com
  • Or via your university’s usual data protection or IT service channels when Wurksy AI is deployed by your institution.

What this notice covers

This notice applies to:

  • The Wurksy AI web application and admin interface.
  • Any support or helpdesk communication directly related to the Service.

It does not cover third party systems that Wurksy AI may integrate with, such as your university’s virtual learning environment (for example Blackboard, Moodle or similar). Those services have their own privacy notices.

Personal data we collect

The Service is designed to collect only the data needed to deliver a controlled AI “sandbox” and to support academic integrity checks.

Depending on how your institution configures the Service, we may process:

Account and identity details

  • Name and institutional email address.
  • Student or staff ID number.
  • Course, school or department and module or assignment codes.

Usage and session data

  • Session identifiers, timestamps, and information about which assignment a session relates to.
  • Prompts and messages you enter into Wurksy AI and the AI responses returned.
  • System events such as when sessions are created, locked, submitted or exported for staff review.

Content you provide

  • Text from assignment briefs that staff upload for use inside the Service.
  • Lecture materials, notes or files that are uploaded so that Wurksy can answer questions about them.
  • Research queries and metadata relating to research articles you choose to look up through the Service.

Technical data

  • IP address and rough location derived from it.
  • Browser type, device type and basic configuration details.
  • Log files and error reports that help keep the Service secure and reliable.

Special category data

Wurksy AI is not intended for processing special category data such as health information or details about your protected characteristics. Please avoid entering this type of information into the Service unless your institution specifically instructs you to do so and has a clear legal basis.

How we use your data and legal bases

The exact legal basis will depend on how your institution deploys Wurksy AI. Typical legal bases under UK data protection law are:

  • Public task – for universities carrying out their teaching, assessment and quality assurance responsibilities.
  • Contract – to provide the Service to you where it is part of your learning or employment environment.
  • Legitimate interests – to ensure fair assessment, maintain academic integrity, prevent misuse of AI tools and improve the Service.
  • Consent – only where you are clearly asked for and give your consent for a specific purpose.

We use your data to:

  • Authenticate you and link sessions to the correct assignment.
  • Enforce assignment specific AI rules, such as prompt caps and output limits.
  • Provide AI assisted suggestions and summaries in line with the assignment policy.
  • Log AI usage and generate an AI Index file that can be attached to your assessment submission.
  • Support staff in reviewing AI usage for academic integrity checks.
  • Monitor usage levels, performance and security of the Service and plan capacity.
  • Investigate and respond to misuse, technical incidents or suspected academic misconduct.

AI providers and data sharing

Wurksy AI uses third party AI providers to generate responses to your prompts. These providers currently include OpenAI or Microsoft Azure OpenAI, depending on how your institution configures the Service.

We share only the text needed to generate a response, for example the prompt you entered, any relevant lecture extracts and system instructions. Where possible, we configure these providers so that:

  • Your data is not used to train or improve their general models.
  • Retention periods are limited to what is needed for security.

We may also share data with:

  • Your university or college, including module leaders and academic integrity staff, so they can review AI usage linked to an assignment.
  • Service providers that host our databases and infrastructure, such as Supabase and cloud hosting platforms.
  • Professional advisers and regulators where required by law or to manage legal claims.

We do not sell your personal data and we do not use it for targeted advertising.

International transfers

Some of our service providers may be located outside the United Kingdom. Where this is the case, we will take steps to ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as standard contractual clauses approved under UK data protection law or use of UK based hosting regions where available.

How long we keep your data

Retention periods are usually set by your institution’s assessment and quality assurance policies.

  • AI session logs and AI Index files are generally kept for at least the duration of the module and any academic appeals period.
  • System logs used for security and performance monitoring may be kept for shorter periods.

Data may be anonymised and kept for longer for reporting, statistical and service improvement purposes. Once anonymised it will no longer be possible to identify you from that data.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have rights in relation to your personal data. These include:

  • Right to be informed about how your data is used.
  • Right of access to a copy of your personal data.
  • Right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data about you.
  • Right to request erasure in some circumstances, for example where data is no longer needed.
  • Right to restrict or object to certain types of processing, especially where based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to data portability for information you have provided, where processing is based on consent or contract and is carried out by automated means.

To exercise your rights you should usually contact your university or college as the data controller for institutional deployments. For the standalone demo Service, you can contact info@wurksyai.com.

Security

We use technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit, access controls, audit logs and regular monitoring of the Service. No online service can be completely secure, but we work to maintain a level of security that is appropriate to the risks.

You are responsible for keeping your university account credentials safe and for following your institution’s acceptable use policies.

Cookies and similar technologies

Wurksy AI uses a small number of cookies or similar technologies to make the Service work and to remember your theme or session state. These are strictly necessary for the Service.

We do not use third party advertising cookies. Your institution may use separate analytics tools on their own websites and learning platforms. Those are covered by their own cookie and privacy notices.

Complaints

If you are unhappy with how your data is being used, you should first contact your university or college or the Operator using the contact details above. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

Details of how to raise a concern with the ICO are available on their website.

Changes to this notice

We may update this privacy notice from time to time. If the changes are significant, reasonable efforts will be made to highlight the update, for example by displaying a notice in the Service. The “last updated” date at the top of this page shows when this notice was last revised.
